Starting in 2016 I discovered a large part of being involved in the party begins with just showing up, and doing so has a tremendous impact on its processes. Since then, I've applied myself to areas of interest where I feel I can make a difference. One simply cannot overstate the importance of involvement, and if you’re interested in changing our politics for the better and want to get involved, know there are likely open positions for leadership, right now, wherever you live, and I will gladly help you to find a position that interests you by sending an email at michael@bearfootformn.com.

I've been a voracious politics geek for years: I breathed politics, but being on the margins and being unfamiliar with party politics I never pictured it as something that was for me. After moving back to Minnesota in 2011 I became a delegate to the State Convention, an experience that proved instructive for the presidential politics of 2016, where Senator Bernie Sanders’ campaign fired me up with a passion and commitment for change. As a result I served as a national delegate for Sen. Sanders to the Democratic National Convention in Philadelphia. From these experiences, one of the most influential and lasting ideas was realizing the importance of getting involved within the party. The DFL, and Democrats generally, aren’t an “other” making decisions; WE are the members, WE are the party, and taking seriously the responsibility I feel to genuinely apply my commitment to improving the lives of fellow citizens—people we know and people we do not know, alike—is through impressing our values upon the Democratic Party as engaged leaders. In other words, improving party politics means having a seat at the table as active, thoughtful members. Running for DNC delegate is a one small, important way to assure my communities have a direct voice in representation.
